English

Short Bio

I am professor in Contemporary Bulgarian Grammar (morphology, syntax and corpus linguistics) in the Faculty of Slavic Studies at Sofia University “St. Kl. Ohridski” and senior researcher in the area of Language Technologies in the Department of AI and Language Technologies at the Institute of Information and Communication Technologies, Bulgarian Academy of Sciences. My scientific interests are in the fields of formal and computational linguistics, language resources, grammar-dictionary interface.

I was a key person in a number of EU projects, related to eLearning, Machine Translation, Language resources (EuroMatrixPlus, AsIsKnown, LTfLL, QTLeap, EUCases, among others). I am the responsible person for the language resources in CLaDA-BG – the CLARIN and DARIAH joint framework in Bulgaria.

I specialized in computational linguistics as a postdoctoral fellow in the Tuebingen University, Germany (2003) - working on the BulTreeBank project, and in Groningen University, the Netherlands (2004) - working on measuring Bulgarian dialects; as a Fulbrighter at Stanford University, the USA (2010) - working on a resource grammar for Bulgarian. I have more than 150 publications in conference proceedings and scientific journals (among which refereed and with SJR factor).

In 2018 I was humbled to receive the award of Clarivate Analytics for excellence in social science research in South-Eastern Europe.
